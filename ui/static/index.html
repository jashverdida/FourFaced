    What is FourFaced?

    You give it a short video. It watches the video, then writes four captions for it — each one in a different voice. We call them the Four Faces:

    01 FormalPolished and professional
    02 SarcasticDry wit, raised eyebrow
    03 Tech humorJokes for computer people
    04 EverydayCasual laughs for anyone

    Pick the caption you like best, copy it, and post it anywhere.

    01 1

    Step 1 · Add your clip

    🎬Your clip drag → 📥Drop box → 📺Preview

    Drag a video file into the dashed box — or click the box and pick a file from your computer.

    No clip handy? Click one of the sample pills below the box.

    You can add up to 4 clips — they line up like characters on a select screen. Click one to bring it to the front:

    clip 1 clip 2 clip 3 clip 4
    02 2

    Step 2 · Caption it

    ✓ FORMAL ✓ SARCASTIC ✓ TECH ✓ EVERYDAY

    All Four Faces are ticked to start. Untick any you don't want — its card below goes dim so you can see who's sitting out.

    ✅Tick Faces → 💬Caption → ⌛~30 sec

    Then press the big Caption button. Each clip takes about half a minute; with several clips they go one after another while you watch the progress line:

    03 3

    Step 3 · Use your captions

    Each Face gets its own card, like this:

    FORMAL

    A sunlit street lined with autumn trees…

    CopyEdit🔊

    Copy puts the caption on your clipboard. Edit lets you change any words (Save keeps them). The speaker icon reads the caption out loud, each Face with its own voice.

    The Evidence box underneath shows what the AI actually saw in your video — so you know the captions aren't made up.

    04 4

    Handy tips

    • The × on a video removes that clip.
    • With several clips, use the ← → arrows, the dots, or click a side clip to switch. The captions shown always belong to the clip in front.
    • Drag more videos anywhere onto the card to add them — no need to hunt for a button.
    • If a clip says FAILED, the others still finish. Just press Caption again.
    • Captions describe what's really on screen — an odd clip gets an odd caption. That's honesty!
    05 5

    The models & the backup plan

    Every caption starts with Gemma 4 31B — a big AI model that can see. It watches your clip, notes what's really there, and writes all four captions.

    🧠Gemma 4 31B keeps failing? → 🛟Gemma 4 26B

    If 31B keeps erroring (with time left in the ~30-second promise), Gemma 4 26B gets one try — at any step: watching, writing, or the final double-check.

    Both couldn't watch the clip?You get a clearly-marked stand-in caption — it never pretends it saw something.
    Watched it, couldn't add the voices?Captions are built from the real notes it already took — plainer, but still true to your video.

    The Evidence box always shows which path — and which model — did the work.

    06 6

    That's the whole book

    Close the manual and go caption something. If a result ever looks off, the Evidence box will tell you exactly what happened — including which model did the work.
